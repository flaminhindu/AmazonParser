1 These filters are horrible
ByOlivia Alexa on March 17, 2013
Item Package Quantity: 1 Verified Purchase
These filters are the sole reason I'll be replacing my humidifier next winter. They used to be much thicker, lasted significantly longer, and cost about $9/filter. The price has (obviously) gone up and the quality has declined. The filters begin to 'calcify' within a few days; the top of the filter hardens and that begins to work its way down (quickly reducing the efficiency of the humidifier) and mold/mildew begins to grow at the bottom of the hardened part about a week and a half into the new filter.

I only use my humidifier about four months each year (during the dry winter months) and take impeccable care of it. I used to go through two filters per 'season' but have used five this season. I would have rather put the money spent on these filters toward a great new (filterless!) humidifier.

1 Thumbs down on Honeywell (KAZ) Humidifier
ByFoggy River on March 7, 2013
Item Package Quantity: 1
KAZ Company, the owner of the Honeywell brand, and manufacturer of Honeywell humidifiers, has a Model HCM-645 unit. Mine came with a Model HAW-504-OS filter and the humidifier would, indeed, put out 4 gallions of water into the air per day. When that original filter had to be replaced the original HAW-504-OS filter part number had been replaced by HAC-504AW. The new filter is much thinner than the original. As a result the humidifier will now put out only about 1.5 gallons per day, a long way from the 4 gallons they promised. I called KAZ looking for some of the original filters. They said the original filter could only be used for factory installation and was not available for replacement use. So I have an expensive humidifier that will not perform as KAZ (Honeywell) advertised. I will not be buying any more KAZ (Honewell) products.
