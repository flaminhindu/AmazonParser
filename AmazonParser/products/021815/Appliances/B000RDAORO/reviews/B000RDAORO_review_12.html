1 I spent extra money to go with a manufacturer replacement ...
BySNJ on December 6, 2014
Item Package Quantity: 1 Verified Purchase
I spent extra money to go with a manufacturer replacement. I have the HCM-515T, one year old. Reading the reviews I was prepared for a thinner, blue coated, 'update' from the manufacture. What I wasn't prepared for is the fact the filter is actually about an 1/8" - 1/4" too high. This slight height difference is not a problem at the back of the humidifier because of a molded radius, integral to the shape of the humidifier, that arcs upward. The radius is mirrored on the tank. Because the radius arcs upward, the tank seat is slightly higher above the filter platform than the front. Now the issue - the front is where the tank connects to the reservoir. The filter height pushes the tank that 1/8" - 1/4" above the reservoir. An 1/8" - 1/4" gap for water, hmmm. The only way to get the tank to fit tight to the reservoir is to cut a filter that was supposedly a manufacture replacement. This is ridiculous. A replacement filter that by its nature will cause the humidifier to leak! First Honeywell creates a replacement filter that is a questionable performer, that we as consumers are stuck with. Then they can't even get their own replacement to fit right in the their humidifiers. I wish I would never have bought a Honeywell, and will not be purchasing Honeywell in the future. Sloppy engineering, period. This will be shared via phone with Honeywell.
